Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to understand, use and manage your emotions in a positive way to relieve stess, communicate effectively, empathise with others, overcome challenges and defuse conflict. It can help you with connecting with your feelings, turn your intention into action and make informed decisions about what matters the most to you.

There are five categories of Emotional Intelligence

    SELF-AWARNESS

    The ability to recognize and understand your own emotions and their impact on others. Emotional self-awareness allows you to identify behavioural and emotional aspects of ourselves which then can be improved.

    SELF-REGULATION

    The ability to manage one's negative or disruptive emotions and adpat to changes in circumstances. Those who are skilled in self-regualtion excel in managing conflict, adapt well to change and are more likely to take responsibility.

    MOTIVATION

    The ability to self-motivate with a focus on achieving internal or self-gratification as opposed to external praise or reward. Individuals who are able to motivate themselves in this way have a tendency to be more committed and goal focused.

    EMPATHY

    The ability to recognise and understand how others are feeling and consider those feelings before responding in social situations. Empathy allows an individual to understand dynamics that influence relationships, both personal and professional.

    SOCIAL SKILLS

    The ability to manage the emotions of others through emotional understanding. These skills are used to build rapport and connection with people through active listening as well as verbal and nonverbal communication.

Whats the difference between EQ and IQ?

EQ (Emotional Quotient) and IQ (Intelligence Quotient) and measurments gained through a standardized test. EQ measures someone's social and emotional competency and IQ someone's intellectual reasoning. Apparently EQ is acquired and can be improved where as IQ is something you're born with (not sure I believe that you're IQ can't be improved or developed).

A person with good EQ can recognise, control and express one's own emotions as well as perceive and assess others. A person with a high IQ can learn, understand and implement knowledge and possess logical reasoning and abstract thinking.
