Identity, Values and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took

As a writer and a poet I perform almost weekly as a one of my main forms of income. A little over a year ago I realized that most of the venues I was performing in weren’t wheelchair accessible. Accessibility (physical, intellectual, financial etc) is something I value a prioritise. I couldn’t continue to perform in spaces that were literally built to exclude a demographic of people, whether that was intentional or not. I also didn’t want to continue participating in artist spaces that didn’t consider the accessibility needs of the Disabled community. I felt it was my responsibility as an able-bodied person to take a stand so I publicly announced I would no longer be accepting gigs that weren’t wheelchair accessible and didn’t have accessible toilets.

I think it’s one thing to tell yourself you’ll commit to something but when you pledge that to a community of people, who watch and hold you to account, it’s a different type of pressure. To tell friends of mine that are wheelchair users that I no longer perform in inaccessible venues puts a lot of emotional pressure to maintain that pledge. It holds me to account. It surprisingly hasn’t impacted a lot of my financial gain from performances but the events I’ve had to turn down have been ‘grassroots’ events. It’s been sad to turn down political and community led events because they’ve lacked the funding to rent out a space that is accessible.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

I once incorrectly promoted a gig and had someone approach me who was upset that they hadn’t been mentioned in the event when they very much were entitled to being listed. It was a very big oversight that I was responsible for and took full responsibility. Even though everything had gone to print and there was nothing I could do to correct what had been done I communicated, apologized, took full responsibility and made sure that in the next round of prints they were mentions as well as across all social media promotions. Taking responsibility didn’t solve the problem but it eased tension. I was disappointed that when I approached my superiors they were very quick to distance themselves from any responsibility and prioritized vocalizing that over problem solving the issue. I think that when you’re working as a team it’s not always important to say who’s at fault as much as it is to find a solution and check in that everyone is feeling on the same page and supported.
